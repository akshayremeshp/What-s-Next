Extra Skills for Students

Marks and degrees matter, but employers hire people who look, sound, and communicate professionally. This module teaches you skills beyond textbooks: LinkedIn, language fluency, and professionalism that set you apart.

Why These Skills Matter

You can be brilliant in academics, but if you can't present yourself professionally, opportunities will pass you by. Companies hire people who:

  • Communicate clearly in English (written and spoken)
  • Have a strong online presence (LinkedIn)
  • Know how to dress, speak, and behave in professional settings
  • Write good emails and resumes

Module 2: Language Fluency (English)

The Reality

  • In India, English fluency is a superpower in careers
  • Interviews are often in English
  • Most tech jobs require English communication
  • Clients, teammates, and managers often expect English proficiency

Four Pillars of English Fluency

1. Speaking

  • Speak clearly without hesitation
  • Confidence even if not perfect grammar
  • Practice with language partners, videos, or voice recording
  • Watch movies, TED talks, YouTube in English

2. Writing

  • Write emails professionally
  • Write clear, concise messages
  • Practice journaling, blogging, or short essays
  • Read good writing to improve your own

3. Reading

  • Read articles, blogs, books in English
  • Expand vocabulary naturally
  • Understand different writing styles

4. Listening

  • Watch English movies, podcasts, YouTube
  • Practice understanding different accents
  • Listen during conversations and meetings

Practical Exercises

  • Daily Reading: Read 1 article in English every morning (10 mins)
  • Speaking Practice: Record yourself speaking for 5 minutes on any topic
  • Writing Practice: Write 1 short paragraph daily (100-150 words)
  • Listening Practice: Watch 1 TED talk or English YouTube video weekly
  • Real Conversation: Talk to an English speaker or join online speaking groups

Module 3: Professionalism & Soft Skills

What is Professionalism?

Being professional means:

  • Dressing appropriately for your context
  • Speaking respectfully to others
  • Being punctual and reliable
  • Listening more than talking
  • Taking responsibility for your mistakes
  • Being respectful to everyone (superiors, peers, juniors)

Dress Code Basics

  • Interviews: Formal shirt + pants/skirt, clean shoes, minimal jewelry
  • Office: Business casual – no torn clothes, no loud colors
  • Client meetings: Formal – blazer, tie, or formal dress
  • General rule: When in doubt, dress one level more formal than the situation requires

Email Communication

  • Subject: Clear and specific (not "Hi" or "Question")
  • Greeting: "Hi [Name]," or "Dear [Name],"
  • Body: Professional but friendly. Be concise.
  • Closing: "Thanks," or "Best regards," or "Cheers,"
  • Signature: Your full name and contact info

Interview Behavior

  • Arrive 10 minutes early
  • Offer a firm handshake and smile
  • Make eye contact
  • Sit upright, don't slouch
  • Listen to the full question before answering
  • Thank the interviewer at the end

Social Media Professionalism

  • Your social media is part of your brand
  • Employers often check Instagram, Twitter, Facebook
  • Avoid posts that are offensive, crude, or controversial
  • Keep private content private
  • Think: "Would I be okay with my boss seeing this?"
